About MCF

Methodist Campus Fellowship is the campus arm of the youth work department that is born out of deep passion for Christ and has become a veritable instrument for positive change in our campuses, church and society at large. We provide spiritual guidance, fellowship, and support for students to deepen their faith while navigating the challenges of campus life.

The fellowship is bent on preaching, teaching and living out the unadulterated gospel of Christ. Our core values include:

  • Faith and spiritual growth
  • Community and fellowship
  • Service and outreach
  • Biblical teaching and discipleship
  • Integrity and accountability
  • Inclusivity within the Christian faith

Our vision is to revive the Wesleyan heritage and possess our generation for Christ. We aim to raise godly leaders who will impact their campuses, communities, and the nation through spiritual excellence and Christ-centered character.

Membership & Participation

Absolutely! The fellowship is home for every young believer who desires to know the Lord better and have a strong community of like-minded saints. While we are rooted in Methodist traditions and the Wesleyan Heritage, we welcome students from all Christian denominations who share our vision and values.

The fellowship is in over 120+ campuses in Nigeria and the work is increasing. If you can't find an MCF chapter on your campus, please contact our National Secretariat at mcfnational@gmail.com or call +234 705 092 4244. We can help connect you to the nearest chapter or provide guidance on starting a new chapter at your institution.

To start an MCF chapter on your campus:

  1. Gather at least 5-10 interested students
  2. Contact the MCF National Secretariat for guidance
  3. Connect with a local Methodist Church for support
  4. Complete the necessary campus registration requirements
  5. Request orientation and resources from the National Executive

Our team will guide you through every step of establishing a vibrant MCF presence on your campus.

Activities & Programs

MCF organizes various spiritual and social activities including:

  • Weekly worship services and Bible studies
  • Prayer vigils and spiritual retreats
  • Annual Believers' Conference
  • Leadership training workshops
  • Community outreach and evangelism
  • Academic excellence programs
  • Social events and fellowship gatherings
  • Zonal and national conferences

Believers' Conference is our annual national gathering that brings together MCF members from all zones across the country. It features powerful teaching sessions, worship experiences, workshops, and networking opportunities. The conference is designed to deepen faith, build connections, and equip students for effective Christian living on campus and beyond.

You can support MCF financially through:

  • Online donations via our Give Online platform
  • Direct bank transfers to the MCF National account
  • Sponsoring specific events or initiatives
  • Contributing to our scholarship fund for members
  • Supporting mission outreach programs

All contributions help us expand our impact and sustain our ministry across campuses nationwide.

Structure & Organization

MCF operates with a structured organization at multiple levels:

  • Local Chapters: Campus-based fellowships led by student executives
  • Zones: Regional groups of chapters for coordinated activities
  • National Executive: Elected officers who provide leadership for the entire fellowship
  • National Secretariat: Administrative headquarters located at Wesley College, Elekuro Ibadan

This structure ensures effective coordination and support for all MCF activities nationwide.
